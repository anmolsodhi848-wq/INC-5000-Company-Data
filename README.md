# **INC-5000-Company-Data**

## **Project Objective**

To analyze and visualize company performance across revenue, growth, workforce, industry, and geographic dimensions using Excel and Tableau to identify key business trends and insights.


## **Dataset Source**

https://github.com/anmolsodhi848-wq/INC-5000-Company-Data/blob/main/INC%205000%20Companies.xlsx


## **Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)**

1. **Total Revenue Generated-** Indicates overall market size and economic impact.
2. **Average Growth Rate-** Highlights strong expansion, especially among high-growth and emerging companies.
3. **Total Number of Companies-** Ensures broad industry and geographic representation.
4. **Total Workforce Size-** Reflects employment contribution and organizational scale.
5. **Top Revenue-Generating Companies-** Prime Therapeutics, Uber Technologies, Allied Universal lead in revenue contribution.
6. **Top Workforce Expansion Leaders-** Allied Universal, Alorica, Infiniti HR show the highest employee growth.
7. **Industry Concentration-** Business Products & Services, IT, and Consumer Products dominate both revenue and company count.
8. **Geographic Concentration-** States like California, Texas, and New York host the highest number of companies.


## **Process**

1. **Data Cleaning & Preparation-** Removed duplicates, standardized industry names, handled missing revenue/workforce values, and converted metrics into consistent units (billions, percentages).
2. **Data Transformation & Calculations-** Created derived fields such as Growth %, Workforce Growth, Average Company Age, and sector-wise aggregations using pivot tables.
3. **Data Visualization-** Built interactive dashboards using bar charts, treemaps, scatter plots, maps, and KPIs to analyze revenue, growth, workforce, industry, and geography.
4. **Insights & Dashboard Design-** Combined multiple views into thematic dashboards (Growth & Revenue, Workforce Expansion, Industry & Geography, Company Longevity) for executive-level storytelling and decision                                      support.

## **Project Insights**

1. **Revenue is Highly Concentrated Among a Few Large Players-** A small set of companies (e.g., Prime Therapeutics, Uber Technologies) contribute a disproportionately large share of total revenue, indicating market concentration at the top.
2. **High Growth Is Not Always Linked to High Revenue-** Several low-revenue companies show extremely high growth percentages, suggesting early-stage or rapidly scaling firms rather than established enterprises.
3. **Business Products & Services Dominates the Ecosystem-** This sector has the highest number of companies and strong revenue contribution, making it the backbone of the overall industry landscape.
4. **Technology and Consumer-Focused Sectors Drive Innovation-** IT, Software, and Consumer Products & Services display strong growth trends, reflecting digital adoption and changing consumer behavior.
5. **Workforce Expansion Mirrors Business Scaling-** Companies with aggressive growth strategies show significant workforce increases, particularly in HR services, logistics, and outsourcing firms.
6. **Geographic Clustering in Economic Hubs-** States like California, Texas, and New York host the largest number of companies, highlighting the importance of established business and innovation ecosystems.
7. **Metro Areas Outperform Non-Metro Regions-** Metro regions such as Minneapolis, San Francisco, and Greater Los Angeles generate higher revenues, indicating better access to talent, infrastructure, and markets.
8. **Younger Companies Exhibit Higher Growth Volatility-** Firms with fewer years on the list show wider growth fluctuations, while older companies demonstrate more stable but moderate growth patterns.
9. **Healthcare and Consumer Services Are Revenue-Strong Sectors-** These industries contribute significantly to total revenue despite having fewer companies compared to service-heavy sectors.
10. **Scalability Over Longevity-** High growth is driven more by scalability and market demand than by company age, as several younger firms outperform long-established organizations.
